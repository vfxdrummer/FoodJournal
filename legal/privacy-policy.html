Privacy Policy

Restaurant Journal · Effective date: July 8, 2026

Restaurant Journal ("the app", "we", "us") helps you remember where you've dined. This policy explains what information the app handles, where it is processed, and the choices you have. It is written to be read, not just to satisfy lawyers.

The short version: Your photos, voice notes, and the faces the app detects are processed on your device and are never uploaded to us. You can use the entire journal without creating an account. An account is required only if you choose to connect a payment card, and your bank credentials are never seen by us or the app — they are handled by Plaid.

1. Information processed on your device only

The following never leaves your phone and is not sent to our servers:

  • Photos and photo metadata. With your permission, the app reads your photo library and analyzes images on-device (using Apple's Vision framework) to detect likely restaurant visits. It uses photos' embedded location (geotags) and timestamps to group visits. Your photos are not copied to or stored on our servers.
  • Voice notes and transcripts. Recordings you make are stored locally and transcribed using Apple's on-device speech recognition where available.
  • Detected faces. If you use the People feature, faces are detected and grouped on-device to let you see who you've dined with. No names or identities are collected, and this face data is not uploaded.
  • Your journal. Visits, notes, occasions, and cover-photo choices are stored locally on your device.

2. Location

The app uses location in two ways: (a) it reads location embedded in your photos to figure out where a visit happened, and (b) with your permission, it may use your device's current location to center the map on you. Coordinates may be sent to Apple (via MapKit) to identify nearby restaurants and to convert coordinates into place names. We do not track your location in the background for advertising or profiling.

3. Account information (optional)

You can create an account using your phone number, Sign in with Apple, or Google. Depending on the method, we receive and store a phone number and/or a name, email address, and profile photo. Account data is stored using our authentication and database provider (Supabase). Phone verification codes are sent via Twilio. Sign in with Apple may provide only a private relay email if you choose.

4. Financial information (only if you connect a card)

If you choose to connect a payment card, we use Plaid to access your transactions. Important points:

  • You enter your bank credentials directly into Plaid's secure interface. We and the app never see or store your bank login.
  • Plaid provides us with transaction data (merchant name, amount, date, category, and general location). We keep only dining-related transactions, to create dining entries.
  • The Plaid access token used to fetch your transactions is stored on our server side and is never placed on your device.
  • Cached dining transactions are stored in our database, protected so that only your account can access them.

5. AI-assisted answers

If you use the "Ask" feature, information from your journal relevant to your question — such as restaurant names, dates, your notes, and voice-note transcripts — is sent to an AI provider (Anthropic and/or OpenAI) to generate a response. This happens only when you ask a question. If you supply your own AI provider key, requests use your key directly; otherwise they pass through our processing service. We do not use your journal to train AI models.

6. Establishment logos

To display a restaurant's logo, the app may send the establishment's website domain to a logo provider (Brandfetch). No personal information is included in these requests.

7. Service providers

We rely on the following providers, who process data on our behalf for the purposes above:

  • Apple — Maps, on-device Vision/Speech, Sign in with Apple.
  • Google — Google Sign-In (if you choose it).
  • Supabase — account authentication and database hosting.
  • Twilio — sending phone verification codes.
  • Plaid — connecting your card and retrieving transactions.
  • Anthropic / OpenAI — generating "Ask" answers.
  • Cloudflare — routing AI requests.
  • Brandfetch — establishment logos.

8. What we do not do

  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • We do not use third-party advertising or ad-tracking SDKs.
  • We do not upload your photos, voice recordings, or face data.

9. Data retention and your choices

  • On-device data (photos, journal, voice notes, faces) is removed when you delete the app.
  • Deleting a visit moves it to "Recently Deleted," then permanently removes it after 30 days (or immediately if you choose).
  • Your account and financial data: you can disconnect a card and delete your account from within the app, which removes your account, stored Plaid access tokens, and cached transactions from our servers.
  • You can revoke photo, location, microphone, or speech permissions at any time in iOS Settings.

10. Security

Access tokens and secrets are stored server-side and never shipped in the app. Your session credentials are stored in the device Keychain. Data in transit is encrypted (HTTPS). No system is perfectly secure, but we design to keep sensitive data off the device and out of the client.

11. Children

Restaurant Journal is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your region), and we do not knowingly collect their information.

12. Your regional rights

Depending on where you live (for example, under GDPR or the CCPA/CPRA), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or port your personal information, and to opt out of certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at the address below.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by updating the effective date above and, where appropriate, notifying you in the app.
